About Me

I am a Graduate Research Scholar at Tsinghua University specializing in quantum algorithms and their applications in physics. My research focuses on Hamiltonian simulation (including SQD and Krylov time-evolution) and computational methods for differential equations. I am also exploring the intersection of physics and AI through quantum machine learning and Hamiltonian-based embedding methods.

My academic journey has been shaped by research experience at DRDO and mentorship under Dr. Xiongfeng Ma, Dr. Amitansu Pattanaik, and Dr. Kathiravan Srinivasan.

Beyond the lab: I am an amateur radio astronomer. I operate a personal 3-meter radio dish for pulsar detection currently I am trying to develop custom algorithm to isolate deep-space signals from PSR B0329+54 Pulsar.

Projects

Track ML

Track ML is a machine learning project focused on particle tracking and event reconstruction in high-energy physics experiments. The project leverages advanced deep learning architectures to improve the accuracy and efficiency of identifying particle trajectories from detector data, contributing to faster and more reliable scientific discoveries.

Surface Code Reliability Validation

Design, Analysis and Validation of Reliable Computing Systems
This project presents a structured review and simulation of Google Quantum AI’s 2023 breakthrough on surface code error correction. It covers experimental and simulated fault injection, reliability modeling, and validation strategies for logical qubits. The work demonstrates how increasing code distance suppresses logical errors, and how fault injection techniques validate system reliability—directly connecting quantum error correction to classical reliability engineering.
